받침 Complete Guide
Final consonant system
The consonant at the bottom of a Korean syllable block — affects pronunciation, sound changes, and grammar particle choice.
Korean syllables are stacked blocks: initial consonant + vowel + optional 받침. The 받침 sits at the bottom and changes pronunciation and grammar rules.

Why 받침 matters
Pronunciation
받침 consonants are unreleased — your mouth closes but no air puff is released
Grammar Particles
은/는, 이/가, 을/를 all change form based on whether a 받침 is present
Sound Changes
받침 triggers 4 major pronunciation changes when followed by certain consonants or vowels
Spelling
Knowing 받침 rules prevents the most common Hangul spelling mistakes
All 27 possible 받침 consonants reduce to just 7 representative sounds at the end of a syllable before a pause or another consonant. Every 받침 you encounter sounds like one of these 7.

The 3 stops [ㄱ] [ㄷ] [ㅂ] are pronounced without releasing air — your mouth closes but no puff comes out. Very different from how English endings are pronounced.
받침 pronunciation changes based on what follows it. There are 4 major rules. Knowing them separates robotic Korean from natural spoken Korean.
연음 — Liaison / LinkingMost frequent
받침 + vowel-starting syllable → 받침 moves to next syllable
밥이→[바비] 한국어→[한구거] 읽어요→[일거요] 앉아요→[안자요]
비음화 — NasalizationVery common
ㄱ/ㄷ/ㅂ 받침 + ㄴ or ㅁ → becomes ㅇ/ㄴ/ㅁ
국민→[궁민] ㄱ→ㅇ 밥물→[밤물] ㅂ→ㅁ 닫는→[단는] ㄷ→ㄴ 한국말→[한궁말]
격음화 — AspirationWith ㅎ
ㄱ/ㄷ/ㅂ/ㅈ + ㅎ → ㅋ/ㅌ/ㅍ/ㅊ (ㅎ disappears)
입학→[이팍] ㅂ+ㅎ=ㅍ 좋다→[조타] ㅎ+ㄷ=ㅌ 축하→[추카] ㄱ+ㅎ=ㅋ
유음화 — Lateralizationㄴ meets ㄹ
ㄴ + ㄹ or ㄹ + ㄴ → both become ㄹㄹ
설날→[설랄] 신라→[실라] 전략→[절략] 일년→[일련]
받침 is the reason Korean has two forms of most particles. Entirely predictable — no exceptions once you know the rule.
FunctionAfter 받침After vowelExamples

Once you internalize the 받침 rule, particle choice becomes automatic. This one rule eliminates some of the most noticeable beginner errors in Korean.
겹받침 (double batchim) = two consonants at the bottom of one syllable. Before a consonant: one is dropped. Before a vowel: the right one moves to the next syllable.
